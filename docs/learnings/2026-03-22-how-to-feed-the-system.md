## Lesson
You shouldn't be writing to knowledge files manually — telling the AI to write them is the workflow.

## What happened
I had built a structured learning system (lessons.md, decisions/, learnings/) but wasn't sure how to actually populate it. The instinct was to write to the files myself, which creates friction and means it doesn't happen consistently.

The actual model: during a session, if something is worth capturing, say "add that to learnings" or "write an ADR for this." I write the file. At the end of any meaningful session, say "let's compound." I review what happened, surface what's worth saving, write the files, and report back.

## Why it matters
The friction of maintaining a knowledge system is what kills it. If you have to write the files yourself, it becomes a chore and it stops happening. If you just have to say one phrase, it happens every time.

This is a broader principle about working with AI: the AI should be doing the writing, including the meta-writing about the work itself. Your job is to have the conversation and make the judgment calls about what matters. My job is to capture it.
